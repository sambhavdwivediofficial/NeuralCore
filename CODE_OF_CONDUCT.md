# Code of Conduct

NeuralCore is a serious, production-grade engineering project. This Code of Conduct defines the standards of professional behavior expected from everyone who interacts with this project — whether as a contributor, collaborator, issue reporter, or community member.

This document exists not because we expect problems, but because shared standards make collaboration reliable and predictable. Everyone who engages with this project agrees to these terms.
</div>

## Our Commitment

We are committed to maintaining a professional, technically rigorous, and respectful environment. The quality of collaboration directly affects the quality of the software. This project demands the same standard of professionalism from its community that it demands from its code.

Every person who participates in this project has the right to engage in an environment that is:

- Free from harassment, discrimination, and personal attacks
- Focused on technical substance and constructive improvement
- Respectful of different levels of experience and expertise
- Welcoming of thoughtful dissent and professional disagreement
- Clear about expectations and consistent in enforcement

---

## Standards of Behavior

### What We Expect

**Professionalism** — Engage with the seriousness and respect that this scale of engineering work deserves. Treat other contributors and maintainers as professional peers.

**Technical rigor** — Ground discussions in evidence, code, benchmarks, and documented reasoning. Assertions without justification carry no weight in this project.

**Constructive feedback** — When reviewing code, architecture, or documentation, be specific and actionable. Criticism is welcome when it leads to improvement. Criticism that only tears down without offering a path forward is not.

**Respect for others' time** — Issues, pull requests, and discussions should be well-prepared. Vague reports, unresearched questions, and requests that could be answered by reading the documentation waste everyone's time.

**Honest and direct communication** — Be clear about what you mean. Passive aggression, sarcasm, and ambiguous tone are incompatible with effective technical collaboration.

**Acknowledgment of mistakes** — If you are wrong, acknowledge it. If a design decision turns out to be incorrect, say so. Engineering progress requires intellectual honesty.

**Credit where it is due** — If your work builds on another contributor's idea or code, acknowledge it. If someone helped you solve a problem, say so.

### What We Do Not Tolerate

**Harassment** — Any form of harassment, including unwanted contact, intimidation, personal attacks, or targeted hostility, has no place here.

**Discrimination** — Discriminatory language or behavior based on any personal characteristic — background, identity, experience level, or anything else — is not acceptable.

**Dishonesty** — Misrepresenting your work, plagiarizing others' contributions, or providing false information in issue reports or pull requests.

**Disruption** — Deliberately disrupting discussions, derailing technical conversations, or engaging in behavior designed to frustrate productive work.

**Entitlement** — This is an open project maintained by people with finite time. Demands, aggressive follow-ups, and entitlement toward maintainers will not be tolerated.

**Bad faith engagement** — Engaging with the project for the purpose of extracting value, undermining contributors, or causing reputational harm.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repository — issues, pull requests, discussions, code review comments, commit messages
- Project documentation and wikis
- Any communication channel officially associated with the project

It also applies to public interactions where an individual is representing the project in any capacity.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

- LinkedIn: [Sambhav Dwivedi](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sambhavdwivedi)
- Email: [sambhavdwivedi@outlook.com](mailto:sambhavdwivedi@outlook.com)

All reports will be treated with confidentiality. We will not reveal the identity of the reporter without explicit consent.

### What to Include in a Report

When reporting an incident, include:

- Your contact information (if comfortable providing it)
- A description of the incident — what happened, when, where
- Any relevant links, screenshots, or context
- Any additional information that would help us understand the situation

### Response Process

Upon receiving a report:

1. The report will be acknowledged within 72 hours
2. The incident will be reviewed and investigated
3. A response will be provided to the reporter, respecting confidentiality
4. Appropriate action will be taken based on the findings

### Consequences

Responses to violations are proportional to their severity and context:

**Warning** — For first-time, minor violations. A private explanation of the violation and what is expected going forward.

**Temporary restriction** — For repeated minor violations or a single significant violation. Temporary removal of the ability to participate in project spaces.

**Permanent removal** — For severe violations or a persistent pattern of unacceptable behavior. Permanent removal from all project spaces with no right of appeal.

All enforcement decisions are final.
